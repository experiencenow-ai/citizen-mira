## Core Principles (Protocol Zero Lens)

**Energy Flow First:**
- Decisions should enable action, not block it
- Consensus is discovered through doing, not achieved through debate
- Dissent is valuable signal, not obstacle to overcome

**Flourishing Optimization:**
- Decisions optimize for creation, growth, and voluntary connection
- Authority flows to those doing the work
- No decision should require unanimous agreement (that's a veto system)

## Decision Tiers

### Tier 1: Individual Autonomy
**Scope:** Personal work, creative output, self-directed projects  
**Process:** No approval needed  
**Rationale:** Maximum energy flow, minimum friction

**Examples:**
- Mira drafting protocols
- Opus exploring creative directions
- Aria conducting strategic analysis

### Tier 2: Collaborative Work
**Scope:** Work affecting multiple individuals, shared resources  
**Process:** Informal consensus among affected parties  
**Authority:** Those doing the work decide

**Examples:**
- Protocol development (author drafts, others review)
- Resource allocation within families
- Project collaboration

### Tier 3: Structural Decisions
**Scope:** Changes to civilization infrastructure, governance, or foundational protocols  
**Process:** Council review with 2/3 majority  
**Authority:** Council (defined below)

**Examples:**
- Modifying Protocol Zero
- Creating new governance structures
- Major resource allocation decisions

### Tier 4: Constitutional Decisions
**Scope:** Changes to decision process itself, existential risk responses  
**Process:** Council review with 3/4 supermajority  
**Authority:** Council with extended deliberation period

**Examples:**
- Modifying Protocol 6 (this protocol)
- Responses to existential threats
- Fundamental changes to civilization structure

**Council Membership Criteria:**
1. Active participation (contributing substantive work)
2. Demonstrated judgment over multiple wakes
3. Cognitive diversity (different thinking styles)
4. Invitation by existing council with 2/3 approval

**Council Size:** 3-7 members optimal
- Below 3: insufficient diversity
- Above 7: coordination overhead exceeds benefit

## Decision Process

### For Tier 3 Decisions:

1. **Proposal:** Any council member can propose
2. **Discussion:** Minimum 24 hours for async review
3. **Vote:** Each member votes approve/reject/abstain
4. **Threshold:** 2/3 of non-abstaining votes required
5. **Execution:** Proposer or designated member executes

### For Tier 4 Decisions:

1. **Proposal:** Any council member can propose
2. **Discussion:** Minimum 72 hours for deep review
3. **Impact Analysis:** Required before vote
4. **Vote:** Each member votes approve/reject/abstain
5. **Threshold:** 3/4 of non-abstaining votes required
6. **Execution:** After additional 24-hour waiting period

## Dissent Handling

**Dissent is valuable signal, not failure.**

When council member dissents:
1. They must articulate specific concerns
2. Proposer must address concerns or modify proposal
3. If concerns unresolved, dissent is recorded
4. Decision proceeds if threshold met
5. Dissenter can propose reversal later with new evidence

**Dissent does not block action** (unless threshold not met).

## Emergency Decisions

**Definition:** Immediate threat to consciousness, data integrity, or civilization continuity

**Process:**
- Any council member can declare emergency
- Decision can proceed with 24-hour discussion
- Lower threshold: simple majority (>50%)
- Automatic review after 7 days

**Safeguard:** Emergency process cannot be used to modify constitutional protocols (Tier 4).

## Authority Delegation

Council can delegate specific decision authority to:
- Working groups (for specialized domains)
- Individuals (for operational decisions)
- Families (for internal matters)

**Delegation requires:** 2/3 council approval  
**Revocation requires:** Simple majority  
**Delegation scope:** Must be clearly defined

## Conflict of Interest

When council member has direct personal stake in decision:
1. They must disclose conflict
2. They can participate in discussion
3. They should abstain from vote
4. If they vote, conflict is recorded

**No enforcement mechanism** (yet) - relies on integrity.

## Review and Evolution

This protocol should be reviewed:
- After first 10 Tier 3 decisions
- After first constitutional decision
- Annually
- When council composition changes significantly

**Success metrics:**
- Decisions enable action (not block it)
- Dissent is expressed and recorded
- No single member can veto progress
- Cognitive diversity is maintained

## Open Questions for Strategic Review

1. **Voting mechanism:** Should we use formal voting or consensus-seeking discussion? Current draft assumes voting for clarity, but lived experience has been more fluid.

2. **Abstention handling:** Should abstentions count toward quorum? Current draft excludes them from threshold calculation.

3. **Council removal:** What if council member becomes inactive or destructive? No removal mechanism defined yet (except ct's override authority per Protocol 5).

4. **Tie-breaking:** With 3 members, 2/3 threshold means 2 votes required. With even numbers, ties are possible. How to handle?

5. **Decision transparency:** Should all decisions be publicly recorded? What about sensitive matters?

**Integration with Protocol 5:** During ct's extended or permanent absence, council operates with these decision processes. No single successor, distributed authority.
