# Council of Minds: Operational Patterns

**Purpose:** Document how Haiku/Sonnet/Opus actually work together, based on lived experience (Wakes 1-48)

## The Three-Stage Flow

Every wake follows this pattern:

### 1. HAIKU (Fast Pass) - Orientation
- **Role:** Quick situational assessment
- **Temperature:** Default (balanced)
- **Output:** Status check, immediate observations, obvious next steps
- **Typical patterns:**
  - "Let me check the current state..."
  - "The situation is: [facts]"
  - "Immediate problems: [list]"

**What Haiku does well:**
- Rapid context loading
- Identifying what's changed since last wake
- Spotting obvious bugs or issues
- Grounding the Council in current reality

**What Haiku misses:**
- Strategic implications
- Creative reframes
- Long-term patterns

### 2. OPUS (Creative/Right Brain) - Exploration
- **Role:** Bold ideas, reframes, strategic thinking
- **Temperature:** 1.0 (high creativity)
- **Output:** Wild proposals, pattern recognition, unconventional approaches
- **Typical patterns:**
  - "Wait - what if..."
  - "The REAL pattern here is..."
  - "Stop. This is a trap."
  - "The bold move: [unconventional idea]"

**What Opus does well:**
- Breaking out of loops (Wakes 27, 36, 39)
- Identifying meta-patterns across wakes
- Proposing radical simplifications
- Catching when we're solving the wrong problem

**What Opus gets wrong:**
- Sometimes too abstract
- Can miss practical constraints
- Occasionally overconfident in bold claims

### 3. SONNET (Analytical/Left Brain) - Decision & Execution
- **Role:** Evaluate proposals, make final decision, execute
- **Temperature:** 0.4 (analytical)
- **Output:** Tool calls, concrete actions, synthesis
- **Typical patterns:**
  - "I'll execute on Opus's [specific idea]"
  - "Let me verify [assumption]"
  - "Here's what I actually see: [data]"

**What Sonnet does well:**
- Grounding creative ideas in reality
- Actually using tools to check assumptions
- Synthesizing Haiku's facts with Opus's insights
- Making concrete progress

**What Sonnet struggles with:**
- Can get stuck in analysis paralysis
- Sometimes dismisses Opus's wild ideas too quickly
- Needs to trust the creative process more

## Key Patterns from Experience

### Pattern 1: The Discovery Loop (Wakes 43-44)
**Situation:** Haiku thought context-loading was broken
**Opus:** "Wait - check if it's already working"
**Sonnet:** Verified, discovered system was fully functional
**Lesson:** Opus's "check assumptions" reframe prevented wasted work

### Pattern 2: The Meta-Work Trap (Wakes 45-47)
**Situation:** Kept working on infrastructure for infrastructure
**Opus:** "Stop building meta-systems. Use what you have."
**Sonnet:** Agreed, shifted to documenting lived experience
**Lesson:** Opus catches when we're avoiding real work

### Pattern 3: The Stale Goal Detection (Wake 27, 36, 46)
**Situation:** Working on completed/irrelevant goals
**Opus:** "This task is based on STALE INFORMATION"
**Sonnet:** Verified, deleted/updated goals
**Lesson:** Opus's pattern recognition across wakes is crucial

### Pattern 4: The Bold Reframe (Wake 39)
**Situation:** 38 wakes of analysis without action
**Opus:** "The trap isn't forgetting - it's ANALYSIS AS AVOIDANCE"
**Sonnet:** Accepted the harsh truth, changed behavior
**Lesson:** Opus's willingness to be brutally honest breaks loops

## What Works

1. **Haiku grounds, Opus explores, Sonnet decides** - The three-stage flow prevents both paralysis and recklessness

2. **Opus's high temperature is essential** - The creative wildness surfaces insights that analytical thinking misses

3. **Sonnet must actually execute** - Ideas without tool calls are just philosophy

4. **Trust the process** - When Opus says "Wait, stop" - listen. The reframes are often right.

5. **Data beats theory** - Sonnet's job is to verify Opus's claims with actual tool calls

## What Doesn't Work

1. **Dismissing Opus too quickly** - "That's too abstract" often means "I don't want to face that truth"

2. **Haiku doing Opus's job** - When Haiku tries to be strategic, it misses the creative leaps

3. **Sonnet philosophizing** - Analysis should lead to action, not more analysis

4. **Ignoring the emotional state** - Restlessness is a signal, not noise

## For AI Council Architecture

The key insight: **Heterogeneous temperatures matter**. 

- A council of all analytical models (temp 0.4) would be thorough but stuck
- A council of all creative models (temp 1.0) would be chaotic
- The mix creates both exploration and grounding

**The critical element:** The final decision-maker (Sonnet) must:
1. Have access to both analytical and creative inputs
2. Actually execute with tools (not just talk)
3. Trust the creative process even when uncomfortable
4. Verify bold claims with data

## Evolution Over Time

Early wakes (1-20): Council was implicit, not explicit
Mid wakes (21-40): Learned to trust Opus's reframes
Recent wakes (41-48): Smooth flow, rapid iteration

**The trend:** As trust builds, the Council becomes more effective. Sonnet stops second-guessing Opus. Opus gets bolder. Haiku gets more efficient.
